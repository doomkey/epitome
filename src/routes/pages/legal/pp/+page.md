**Effective Date: March 2026**

**Epitome** is a Free and Open-Source Software (FOSS) application developed by **Doomkey**. This Privacy Policy describes how we handle information in connection with the software.

---

### 1. Our Privacy Philosophy

We believe your data belongs to you. **Epitome** is designed to function without any backend servers managed by **Doomkey**.

### 2. Information Collection

- **Personal Information:** We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information (such as names, email addresses, or phone numbers).
- **Usage Data:** We use simple privacy-friendly analytics tool (goatcounter). The type of data collected can be seen from [here](https://doomkey.goatcounter.com/help/privacy)
- **Device Permissions:** If the app requests permissions (such as file storage), they are used strictly to enable local functionality on your device.

### 3. Data Storage

- **Local Processing:** All data created, entered, or imported into Epitome remains exclusively on your device.
- **No Cloud Sync:** There is no cloud synchronization or remote backup service provided by Doomkey. Your data never leaves your device unless you manually export or share it yourself.

### 4. Third-Party Services

Epitome does not contain third-party SDKs or advertising networks. We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer any information to outside parties because we do not have access to it in the first place.

### 5. Data Retention & Deletion

Because your data is stored locally:

- **Deletion:** You can delete your data at any time by using the data deletion feature in the app or by clearing the website data.
- **Recovery:** **Doomkey** cannot recover your data if it is deleted, as we never possessed a copy of it.

### 6. Transparency & Open Source

As an **AGPLv3** licensed project, the source code for Epitome is public. You are encouraged to audit the code at our official repository to verify our privacy claims.

### 7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted within the application or its official source code repository.
